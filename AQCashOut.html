Cashing-Off Procedures

End of Shift - Cashier and Manager:

  1. Print a drawer balance report from POS. Count the float from total cash, and place the remaining money into a cash envelope.
  2. Ensure drawer blance cash total matches the flap from the cash envelope
  3. Settle the debit/credit terminals (if applicable, see Debit/Credit terminals)
  4. Fill over/short report if required
  5. Staple together, in this order, from top to bottom: Cash envelope flap, recipts, drawer balance report, and over/short report (if applicable). Place in balancing folder/drawer
  6. Place float and cash envelope in the safe
  7. Ensure that the cash drawer is left open. In the case of a break-in, we would rather have people know that there is no money, rather than trying to break into it.

Over/Short Procedures

Over/Short

It is extremely important for all front desk staff to be precise when handling cash. Overages and outages are not acceptable on a routine basis.

Pool managers are expected to clar over/shorts before the day's end. They are then required to inform the Admin Coordinator of the issue.

Should a problem occur, and the issue cannot be cleared, please follow the procedure below:

Yellow

Less than $5

  • Recount Float and Deposit
  • Write descrepancy on recap form

Orange

Less than $10, more than $5

  • YELLOW Procedure, plus:
  • Fill out an over/short report

Red

More than $10

  • ORANGE Procedure, plus:
  • Fill out an over/short report

If overages/outages become an ongoing problem, the Admin Coordinator will assess the situation and implement additional training and/or take disciplinary action. Ongoing development is a key aspect of any front desk job. Please ask for additional training if you are worried about possible overages/outages.
